SERENDIPITY THE NEW DICTIONARY 489
Serendipity /ˌserənˈdɪp.ɪ.ti/
noun The occurrence and development of events by chance in a happy or beneficial way; a fortunate stroke of luck.
• We found the charming little restaurant by pure serendipity.
• A fortunate stroke of serendipity led him to the discovery of penicillin.
Synonyms: fluke, fortuity, providence, happy accident
Antonyms: misfortune, bad luck, design
[Mid 18th century: coined by Horace Walpole from the Persian fairy tale 'The Three Princes of Serendip']
